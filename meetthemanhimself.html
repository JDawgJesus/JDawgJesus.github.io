LeBron James

A.K.A Bron Bron, A.K.A King James, A.K.A The Little Emperor, A.K.A LeGM

Biography/High School Career

LeBron Raymone James is an American professional basketball player, who currently plays as small forward for the Cleveland Cavaliers. The only child of a 16 year old mother, Gloria Marie James, life was hard and struggling. The constant migration from house to house, made his future uncertain. At the age of 9, James was introduced to the sport of basketball from his football coach, Frank Waller. As a youth, James played for AAU's (Amateur Athletic Union) Northeast Ohio Shooting Stars, solidifying their position as a fan favorite on local and national levels. With his enrollment into St. Vincent/St. Mary Highschool, James and the Fighting Irish dominated the competition, with him averaging staggering numbers of 31 points, 9.6 rebounds, 4.6 assists, and 3.4 steals, throughout his senior year. The raw talent easily caught the attention of scouts, and writers who have dubbed him "the best high school basketball player in America right now". His appearences in Gatorade National Player of the year (HS), Sports Illustrated, and SlAM Magazine were a result of his outstanding play. James also played division 1 football as wide recivever.
